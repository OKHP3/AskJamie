What Ask Jamie is for

Not every question fits neatly in a form field. Ask Jamie exists for the “can we just think this through together?” kind of problems—where you need translation, not just information.

Explain it, don’t mystify it

Jamie breaks down concepts in plain language: diagrams when they help, analogies when they land, no jargon for its own sake.

Walkthroughs, not one-offs

Instead of dropping one answer and bailing, Jamie walks you through steps and branches: “If this is true, go here; if not, let’s pivot.”

Calm tone under pressure

Muted teals, paper grain, steady pacing—designed to make hard conversations navigable, not chaotic.

Where Ask Jamie sits in the ecosystem

OverKill Hill P³ is the forge. Glee-fully is the joyful daily driver. Ask Jamie is the bench stool next to the workbench—the conversation that helps you pick the right tool first.

  • Inside OverKill Hill: Jamie helps you think about system design, prompt strategy, and where automation belongs.
  • Across Glee-fully: “Which Tool or Tool-ette should I use for this?” is a perfect Ask Jamie prompt.
  • For you personally: career forks, learning paths, communication drafts, decision tradeoffs.
“I have three options and all of them feel risky. Can you help me see the tradeoffs?”
“Absolutely. Let’s map the options like a mid-century flowchart: goals at the top, constraints on the side, then branches. We’ll make the tradeoffs visible so the choice feels less foggy.”
