About Enterprise Sleuth

Contextual Intelligence Retrieval

Organizational Cartography

What GPT‑AJ03 actually does

Enterprise Sleuth is a lens on your data: one part detective, one part analyst, one part explainer. It’s designed for noisy, real‑world enterprises — not tidy demo datasets.

Thinks like an investigator, not a search box

Classic search answers “where is this file?”. Enterprise Sleuth is built to answer “what’s the story here?” and “what changed over time?”. It pulls fragments from policies, project docs, tickets, chats, and meeting notes, then stitches them into timelines and narratives people can actually act on.

It leans on AskJamie’s three personas — detective, analyst, teacher — to keep answers both useful and human. The detective hunts through the haystack. The analyst connects patterns and trade‑offs. The teacher explains it back in clear language so your team doesn’t have to be “AI people” to benefit.

Under the hood, it’s also a prompt architecture: careful guardrails, reasoning steps, and sanity checks that keep the model from drifting off into hallucinations or unhelpful tangents as it works through complex questions.

Six core capabilities it brings to work

  • Contextual Intelligence Retrieval (CIR) — pulls the right evidence at the right time.
  • Organizational Cartography Engine (OCE) — maps people, teams, and systems over time.
  • Implicit Knowledge Mining (IKM) — surfaces the “unwritten rules” hiding in chatter and docs.
  • Compliance & Governance Correlation (CGC) — lines up decisions with policy and risk context.
  • Adaptive Discovery Orchestration (ADO) — mutates search strategies when the straight line fails.
  • Cognitive Companion Interface (CCI) — keeps the voice steady: professional, analytical, slightly wry.

Bring your own platform. The recipes travel.

Underneath the logos, all modern “agent” platforms do a similar thing: they let you save detailed instructions and attach files so the AI behaves like a reusable, specialized tool. Enterprise Sleuth is designed to map cleanly onto all of them.

Plain‑English tour of the ecosystem

If you’re not living in AI blogs every day, the vocabulary gets overwhelming fast. Here’s the simple view:

  • OpenAI Custom GPTs — your own versions of ChatGPT with saved instructions and up to dozens of attached files. They live inside ChatGPT and can be shared like mini‑apps.
  • Microsoft Copilot Studio Agents — agents you design inside Microsoft 365 so they can work in Teams, SharePoint, and Office. You describe what they should do and what data they can see, and Copilot carries it out.
  • Copilot Studio Lite Agents — a lighter, template‑driven way to spin up simpler agents quickly, often surfaced directly inside Microsoft 365 as “apps in chat.”
  • Google Gemini Gems — “custom experts” you define for Gemini. You give them a name, instructions, and a handful of reference files so they remember how you like things done.
  • Claude Projects & Artifacts — focused workspaces where Claude can use your uploaded docs plus persistent instructions, and then produce reusable outputs (artifacts) like docs, dashboards, or small tools.
  • Perplexity Spaces / Labs — project hubs where you combine deep research, your own uploads, and saved instructions so you can revisit and extend work over time.

Different labels, same basic pattern: a saved “personality and job description” plus a pile of relevant evidence. Enterprise Sleuth gives you the playbook for that pattern so you can stand up the same sleuth across whichever platforms your organization already pays for.

More controllable — never fully predictable

A key reality check: no custom GPT, Copilot agent, Gem, or Claude project is ever 100% predictable. They’re more like talented new hires: you can give them a great job description, a good starter pack of examples, and clear boundaries — and then you manage the relationship.

The Enterprise Sleuth approach narrows the behavior band dramatically with structured instructions, examples, and guardrails, but it never pretends the model is a vending machine. You stay in the loop. We simply make that loop faster, safer, and more consistently useful.

How the Sleuth recipes plug in

  • Start with the same instruction skeleton (identity, mission, rules, workflow, output formats).
  • Drop it into your platform of choice (Custom GPT, Copilot Studio, Gems, Claude, Perplexity).
  • Attach your own knowledge: policies, playbooks, project archives, catalogs, or data exports.
  • Iterate once with your team, then reuse endlessly as a branded, in‑house “AI colleague”.
